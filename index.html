Hok Chun "Anthony" Kwan faces up to five years in a Thai prison for possession of body armor without a license. He was arrested by Thai authorities at Bangkok's Suvarnabhumi Airport on August 23, 2015, trying to travel to Hong Kong after reporting on the aftermath of the August 17 bombing that killed 20 people at a religious shrine.

Body armor, including helmets and flak jackets, are commonly used by frontline journalists in hotzones, though civilian possession of such equipment is illegal under Thai law. Two journalists have been killed in Thailand: 44-year-old Hiroyuki Muramoto, a Japanese cameraman working for Reuters who was shot dead during violent clashes in Bangkok on April 10, 2010, and Italian freelance photographer 45-year-old Fabio Polenghi, who was similiarly killed in May 19, 2010. Neither wore a bulletproof vest.

Kwan was released on bail the following Monday and a Bangkok military court ordered him not to leave the country pending investigation. Kwan's passport was confiscated and he was required to report to the Thai court every 12 days. Kwan is an employee of Hong Kong-based Initium Media Technology.

The Committee to Protect Journalists, Reporters Without Borders and other organizations have condemned Kwan's detainment by the Thai government and Anthony's supporters from across the world are fighting for his release back to Hong Kong.

As of early September, Thai officials returned Anthony's passport, allowing him to return to Hong Kong, while still requiring him to travel to Bangkok every 12 days to check in.

But on October 9, 2015, Thai authorities formally charged Anthony with carrying military weapons without permission, forcing him to appear before the country's court.
